Acknowledgements

The ACM is a student organization and would not exist without the contributions of volunteers over the years! People have given us books and hardware

This page is horribly incomplete and was mostly ported directly from the old website; if you’re not listed here but feel that you should be, please contact us and we’ll be more than happy to add you!

Outstanding Volunteers

There are some individuals who have helped out in huge ways in maintaining the ACM’s systems despite not actually being cursed elected official sysadmin. This section honors those individuals who have poured more than their share of blood, sweat, and tears into maintaining the ACM even when not serving as a current officer.

  • Nathaniel Wes ‘nwf’ Filardo: It’s safe to say that nwf is probably the main reason the ACM’s current systems and infrastructure are standing, having helped build the cluster and acting as probably the longest-serving volunteer sysadmin who was never actually the sysadmin. In addition, he curated and maintained the museum and served as the organization’s institutional memory for many years. His contributions are far greater than we can record in this space and cannot be overstated.
